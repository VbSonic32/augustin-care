Maintenance Categories

Everything Your Lawn Needs

Establishment

The best time to establish warm-season grass is during active growth periods. Plant sod, plugs, or seed when the grass is vigorously growing to ensure strong root development before stressful conditions arrive.

Spring Summer
Mowing

Mow at 2 to 4 inches weekly, or as frequently as necessary to prevent scalping. Never remove more than ⅓ of the leaf tissue at one time. In winter, mow only if needed to prevent weeds from flowering.

Winter Spring Summer Fall
Fertilization

Make the first application when the lawn is actively growing and has been mowed at least twice. Apply 0.5–1 lb of nitrogen per 1,000 ft² up to 4 times during the growing season. Do not exceed 4 lbs N/1,000 ft² per year or apply to stressed grass.

Spring Summer Fall
Aerification

Aerate to relieve soil compaction, especially in new lawns with limited organic matter or in heavily used lawns. Best performed when there is adequate soil moisture — typically spring through early fall.

Spring Summer
Thatch Removal

Remove problematic thatch using hollow-tine aerification, a vertical mower, or a power rake. Thatch at 0.5 to 1 inch depth can begin to impede water infiltration and harbor disease and insects.

Spring Summer
Weed Control

Apply pre-emergence herbicides when soil temps reach ~55°F for 4–5 consecutive days to prevent summer annuals (crabgrass, goosegrass). In fall, apply pre-emergence at ~70°F for winter annuals. Apply post-emergence as needed year-round.

Winter Spring Summer Fall
Irrigation

Turn off irrigation in winter when the grass is dormant. Complete the "Water-Wise Checklist" before turning on in spring. During the season, irrigate only when necessary to prevent wilting or to replace at least 60% of evapotranspiration.

Spring Summer Fall
Insect Control

Apply preventative white grub products if necessary in early summer. Apply curative treatments for white grubs as needed. Scout for chinch bugs (apply insecticide if found) and watch for fall armyworms in late summer through fall.

Summer Fall
Disease Control

Scout for take-all root rot and gray leaf spot during the growing season. Apply preventative fungicide for large patch disease (Rhizoctonia solani) in fall and spring when soil temperatures are between 50–70°F in areas with a history of disease.

Best Practices

Professional Care Secrets

01
The ⅓ Rule

Never remove more than one-third of the leaf blade in a single mowing session to avoid scalping and stress.

02
Soil Test First

Always apply nutrients beyond nitrogen based on soil test results. Don't guess — your soil tells you what it needs.

03
Watch Soil Temp

Pre-emergence herbicides only work when applied at the right soil temperature window — timing is everything.

04
Never Stress-Feed

Do not apply fertilizer to stressed grass. Drought, heat, or disease stress must be resolved before feeding.

05
Water Wisely

Irrigate only to prevent wilting or replace 60% of ET. Skip irrigation during rainy periods and late fall.
